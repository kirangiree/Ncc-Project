Senior Division:

Senior Division

NCC Nepal had conducted 9 batches of Senior Division Training during 8 years of its establishment until 1974,when this training was discontinued due to the changes in the government education policy. However, Senior Division Training continued in 2014 .A total of 1375 cadets have been trained in the Senior Division Training.

Importance of Senior Division Training:

-Development of leadership quality and self confidence.
-To assist in personality development.
-To develop responsibility towards family and society.
-Inspire to become self-dependent.
-To produce trained youth which can be mobilized on post disaster.
-To increase youth in level of awareness on nature/environmental conversation, drugs abuses ,human trafficking etc

Eligibilty:

Student of Higher Secondary Level
Age limit : 16 into 21 years
Height:
-Male cadet most be 5ft
-Female cadet most be 4ft 10inch
Medical test passed
Regular attendance at school

Training:

NCC Nepal provides 100 days training package in which 79 days is institutional training, given at the very location of the schools, whereas remaining 21 days is conducted annual camp. Languages, social, cultural, religious, geographical knowledge etc. and extracurricular activities are interchanged among the cadets who participate in the camp, which results fraternity among them. The training is conducted as follows;

(a) Institutional Phase:

This is the 79 days training given at the very location of schools which includes the basic military trainings. The main aim of this training is to produce a dutiful and disciplined citizen who is physically strong and mentally robust with good personality. Following trainings are provided in this session:

-Physical Training
-Aerobics
-Mass PT
Taekwondo
-Basic Military skill
-Drill
-Khukuri Drill
-Field Craft/ Battle Craft
-Map Reading
-Weapon training and Firing
-Disaster Management
-Introduction of Disaster Management
-Types of disaster prone to Nepal
- DifferenetRole of Volunteers during disaster response

(b) Annual Camp Phase:

The annual camps are conducted in 3 locations Hattikhor of Nawalparasi, Kohalpur of Banke and Yangshila of Morang for 21 days, which consists of following activities:
